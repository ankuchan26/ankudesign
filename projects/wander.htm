Before smartphones,

we moved around cities using different kinds of printed maps. We developed routes tailored to our criteria and matched landmarks and signs in the environment with the map.

Today, technology has overtaken the role of navigation. The introduction of these devices has also shaped our interaction with space towards a passive overview of our surroundings. Several spatial processes have been completely handed over to our smart machines.
